Overview

Flotilla is a naval warfare game between two players. After both players have placed their ships on the board, the game begins. Players will take turns firing ordinance at grid positions. Different ordinance types have different effects (see the ordinance section below). Each shot will be marked as either a hit (red) or a miss (white). At the conclusion of each turn, the ships will all move according to their speed (see the ships section below). The first player to sink their opponent's entire fleet wins.

How to Play

Joining a Game

The first two participants to join a game room are the players and all subsequent participants that join the same room will be set up as observers. Players can see their own ships on the top grid and keep track of their shots on the bottom grid. Observers will see both players ships (player 1 on the top and player 2 on the bottom). All participants can use the chat box to interact with one another. In addition to participant messages, game messages will also be sent to the chat box.

Ships

There are 5 types of ships in the game. Each ship has its own speed and number of hits required to sink as outlined in the table below. The speed indicates how many grid squares the ship will advance after each turn. When a ship reaches the edge of the grid, it will reverse direction 180 degrees -- for instance, if it was travelling up, it will turn so it will travel down. Turning around counts as its movement for that turn.

Icon Ship Name Speed Hits to Sink
Carrier 1 5
Destroyer 2 4
Cruiser 3 3
Submarine 2 3
Corvette 3 2

Ship Placement

When the game first begins, it will be in 'placement' mode. When in this mode, the two players can drag their ships to the desired initial positions on their ship grid (the top-most grid). A ship must be fully on the grid (no part of it may extend beyond the grid surface) for it to be a legal placement. When a ship is on the grid, double-click it to rotate it.

Ships may overlap. Note if a shot hits a grid cell that contains multiple ships all of the ships will be hit.

Once all ships are on the grid, the "Ready" button will appear. After the player is satisfied with how their ships are arranged, click in the ready button. Once the button has been clicked, the ships will not be able to be moved.

Play Mode

Once both players have clicked "Ready" the game transitions to "Play" mode. In this mode, players take turns firing at their opponent. To fire, a user selects the desired ordinance (see below) and then clicks the grid square to target. The grid squares affected by the shot will be colored red if it was a hit and white if it was a miss. Each shot square will also contain a number. This is the number of turns since that shot was fired. Since ships move, a cell that was a miss could become a hit at a later time (or vice versa) so a player may have to fire at the same space more than once. If the cell that was fired upon was a miss but it was a hit on some prior turn, the cell will be colored pink.

As a player's ships are hit, a red square will be shown on top of the ship where it was hit. Once all positions of a ship have been hit, it is sunk. Sunk ships remain on the board but no longer move. Hitting a ship in a position that was already hit (regardless of whether it has been sunk) will always be scored as a miss.

Ordinance

There are 3 types of ordinance available to players. Players have an unlimited number of shells but finite number of other types. See the table below for details.

Type Quantity Effect
Shell Unlimited Hits a single grid cell.
Missile 3 Hits a 3x3 square of cells centered at the cell at which it was fired.
Drone 5 Reveals a 5x5 section of the game board, centered at the cell at which it was fired. The reveled section of the grid will be shaded with a light color. Cells that contain an enemy ship will be shaded darker. The shading fades as turns progress.
